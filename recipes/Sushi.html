Sushi

Description

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that combines vinegared rice, called shari, with various toppings or fillings, known as neta, which can include raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, and sometimes fruits. It is often accompanied by soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger (gari).

Ingredients

  • Sushi rice: Short-grain rice seasoned with vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  • Dried seaweed sheets used to wrap sushi rolls.
  • Fish: Popular options include tuna, salmon, shrimp, and eel, often raw or lightly cooked.
  • Vegetables: Cucumber, avocado, pickled radish, and others.
  • Condiments: Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger for flavor balance.

Steps:

  1. Prepare Rice: Cook sushi rice, season with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.

  2. Prep Fillings: Slice fish, julienne veggies, and prepare nori sheets.

  3. Assemble:
    • Maki rolls:Spread rice on nori, add fillings, roll with a bamboo mat, and slice.
    • Nigiri: Shape rice mounds, top with fish, secure with nori if needed.

  4. Serve: Plate neatly with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
